Article Paper Development

Welcome To Paper Machine

Answer the questions below to develop your research paper.
Once completed, download the responses as a PDF.

Title and Abstract

1. Clearly state the research problem or question.
2. Provide a brief overview of the existing literature.
3. Present the paper's thesis statement or main argument.

General Prompts:
"Generate a research title and an abstract for an article on [insert topic]. The abstract should summarize the research objectives, methodology, key findings, and significance in 200–250 words."

Introduction

1. Conduct a comprehensive review of relevant literature.
2. Identify key theories, concepts, and empirical findings.
3. Highlight gaps in the literature and the need for further research.

General Prompts:
"Write an introduction for a research article on [insert topic]. Include the background of the study, the problem statement, and the objectives of the research. The introduction should be 300–400 words long."

Literature Review

1. Develop a theoretical framework that underpins the paper's argument.
2. Define key concepts and their relationships.
3. Explain how the framework contributes to understanding the research problem.

General Prompts:
"Provide a literature review section for a study on [insert topic]. Summarize and analyze at least five key scholarly sources, highlighting their relevance, gaps, and how they inform the current research. Use proper in-text citations and ensure the section is 600–800 words."

Theoretical and Conceptual Framework

1. Formulate clear and testable propositions or hypotheses.
2. Explain how these propositions or hypotheses relate to the theoretical framework.

General Prompt:
"Develop the theoretical and conceptual framework for a research article on [insert topic]. Explain the theories or models that underpin the study, and outline how these are applied in the research context. Include a diagram to represent the conceptual framework if possible. This section should be 400–500 words."

Future Work and Conclusion

1. Analyze the implications of the theoretical framework.
2. Discuss the strengths and weaknesses of the proposed framework.
3. Consider potential limitations and future research directions.

General Prompts:
"Write a future work section for a study on [insert topic]. Discuss potential research directions, limitations of the current study, and how future studies could expand on the findings. This section should be 200–300 words long."
"Draft a conclusion for a research article on [insert topic]. Summarize the key findings, their implications, and the overall contribution of the study. The conclusion should be 250–300 words."

References

1. Summarize the main findings and contributions of the paper.
2. Reiterate the significance of the research.
3. Offer concluding remarks and potential implications for future research.

General Prompt:
"Generate a reference list in [insert preferred citation style, e.g., APA 7th edition] for the following sources: [list at least five sources or let the student input them]. Ensure the references are formatted accurately."
